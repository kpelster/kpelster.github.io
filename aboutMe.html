About Me

I was born and raised in Omaha, Nebraska until I came to the University of Delaware for school. My early years were spent playing every sport and engaging in creative hobbies. Notably, I kept playing soccer, dance, and golf (fun fact - I have had a hole in one before!). My other hobby, which I enjoy the most outside of school and work, is charcoal drawing. While I am pursuing a degree in computer science, interestingly enough, I never took a computer science course until I started at UD. After being required to take an intro level course and further becoming a lab assistant and teaching assitant, I realized computer science was right for me, and I started my path towards getting a bachelors in Computer Science.

Most of my professional experience has actually been in physical science labs. I worked under a graduate student in the department of Chemical Engineering as a mentee. After that, I spent a few years in a composite materials lab at the Center for Composite Materials.

At last, I began working as a student trainee doing web development for the army at Aberdeen Testing Center in Aberdeen Proving Ground, Maryland. I still work there and I love it! I will be fortunate to be able to start there full time as a Computer Scientist after I graduate in December.

I currently live in Port Deposit, Maryland. It is a quaint little town that I have grown to enjoy thoroughly. I have a dog named Finn, and a cat named Jake. Bonus points if you can name the TV show they are named after ;).
